Boost your well-being staying indoors!



Connect with others


We are social creatures; relationships are key to our mental health. Having to self-isolate or practice social distancing may be difficult. There are obvious ways to digitally connect through WhatsApp, Skype and Facebook.



Physical activity


Our physical and mental health are undeniably linked, so it’s important to stay active, even if we don’t feel like it! Exercise in the available space. Cleaning your own place, washing your clothes, instead of using a machine can also contribute to some form of physical activity!



Mental activity


MEDITATE! Meditation is undoubtedly beneficial and has multiple health related benefits. It can be done alone or with your family. Board games are a good way of keeping yourself mentally active. Or learning a card game? Most of us have a deck of cards at home!



Learning new skills


There are loads of skills you can learn online. Courses for various skills that might help you professionally are available for free of cost in websites such as Coursera, Udemy, etc. Explore your interests! Apart from that, personal interests like drawing, painting, knitting can also be explored.



Plan your day


You tend to utilize time more efficiently when you plan it!  Sit down with a blank piece of paper and decide what will make this day highly successful. Include work, diet, relaxation, connecting with friends, exercise, hobbies etc. Review your day. Check out what worked and what kept you idle.



Reenergize your surrounding


Redecorate your house! Shift your beds, tables, create posters, organize your shelves in a better way. Lit lamps, spend more time with pets, nurture plants and keep yourself occupied.



Stay away from negativity


Isolate yourself from negative news and don’t look out for death tolls, it’s not a cricket match to know the latest score. Avoid sending fatalistic messages. Stay positive.



Self care


When we have a lot of free time, it's easy to slip into the habit of sleeping late, spending all day in your pyjamas and eating junk food, but looking after yourself is essential for your mental health. Even simple tasks such as washing your face can feel difficult sometimes, but they can make a big difference to the way you feel.
